You must know that there were animals in those days that are no longer seen today. The four-tusked elephant, the thirty cubit long crocodile or the grinning three-horned rhinoceros of those days, none of these are found today. Sometimes, on the rocks or under the ice in caves, some traces of their skeletons are found – from which scholars know that such and such beasts once existed on earth. Those who practice all these things can tell by looking at the teeth little by little – what kind of animal’s teeth it is, whether it eats meat or is vegetarian, etc.<\/p>\n

The animal I am talking about is called Sabre-toothed Tiger in English. Its skeletons have been found in Europe, America, our country and many other places. It is difficult to say what he did with these sword-like teeth. It is not convenient to bite with such long teeth; Also, this tiger’s jawbone was not as strong as that of today’s tigers, so its bite force was less.<\/p>\n

The teeth were about six inches long, with knife-like edges\u2014perhaps useful for slicing off prey.
