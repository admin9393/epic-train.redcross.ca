The government’s target for the current financial year is to keep inflation within 6 percent. But the crazy horse of inflation is not being managed at all. In February, the country’s inflation eased slightly from January to 9.67 percent. In January it was 9.86 percent.<\/p>\n

However, Bangladesh Bank has set the inflation target at 7.5 percent in the second half monetary policy.
\nAccording to the latest data of Bangladesh Bureau of Statistics, the country’s inflation rate in February is 9.67 percent. According to the sources of the Ministry of Planning, this updated information is known. Inflation of 9.67 percent means that if the price of a product in February 2023 is Tk 100, then in February 2024 it has to be bought at Tk 109 67 paise.<\/p>\n

At this time, the average price inflation of food in the country is 9.56 percent, while the inflation of non-food products is 9.42 percent. Meanwhile, the inflation rate in rural areas is slightly lower than in urban areas. Rural inflation is 9.48 percent, while urban inflation is 9.88 percent.
